Each week the PlanPhilly staff curates our top picks of the week’s most informative, entertaining, and important events from our events page . Hosting an event? Submit it here and we’ll consider including it in our weekly email.
Before we get to the regular events schedule, here’s a quick plug for our Neighborhood Civics 101 event next week during State of Young Philly. PlanPhilly reporter Jared Brey will moderate a conversation with members of some of Philly’s best-organized neighborhood civic groups about what civics do, what powers they have, and why Young Involved Philadelphians (and any of our readers!) should get involved with them. These are going fast so grab a ticket while they last.
Read on for our picks from this jam-packed week of DesignPhiladelphia and State of Young Philly events:
Monday 10/12
11:00 am: The Art of Active Play family play day at Smith Memorial Playground. Free.
6:00 pm: WHYY hosts a Mayoral debate with Young Involved Philly, Committee of Seventy and several other groups. Free.
Tuesday 10/13
1:00 pm: Alan Greenberger, Paul Levy, Giana Lawrence, Renee Gilinger, and others talk commercial corridors and streetscape transformation at this year’s Corridor Realities panel. $5 - $10.
5:30 pm: Pennsylvania craft and design from 1700 to the present, including works by George Nakashima and Philip Lloyd Powell. Free.
6:30 pm: SOSNA is holding their annual board elections. Free.
7:00 pm: Hawthorne neighbors continue the conversation about Bart Blatstein’s development proposal for Broad and Washington at their general meeting. Free.
Wednesday 10/14
10:00 am: Wonk out on freight policy at DVRPC’s Goods Movement Task Force meeting. Free.
5:00 pm: The Grays Ferry Triangles committee unveils new design plans for a permanent version of the triangle plaza at 23rd and South. Free.
6:00 pm: Signs of Change: Reception + Street Art Panel moderated by Conrad Benner. Pay what you wish.
6:00 pm: The Neighborhood Gardens Trust hosts a public meeting at PHS about securing permanent land access for community gardens. Free.
6:30 pm: I Heart the Arts happy hour at Dilworth Park hosted by WHYY, Philly Te Ama, Visit Philly,, CPDC, Fleisher Art Memorial, and YIP. Free.
Thursday 10/15
5:30 pm: Women in Architecture hosts a Wikipedia Edit-a-thon to improve Wikipedia's content of notable women in architecture. Free.
6:00 pm: Friends of Bainbridge Green and OLIN will lead a walking tour of the would-be Bainbridge Green improvements. Free.
7:00 pm: Fishtown Neighbors Association is accepting nominations for board candidates at their general meeting. Free.
7:30 pm: Queen Village neighbors get an update on the new Headhouse Square plaza plans, and present the board candidates for November’s elections. Free.
Friday 10/16
9:00 am: DVRPC’s Strategies for Older Suburbs roundtable series looks at the benefits of immigration for reail districts. Free.
6:00 pm: The State of Young Philly Opening Night & Civic Engagement Fair at WHYY. Free.
Saturday 10/17
1:00 pm: Snack your way through Chinatown with Philly Food Works and the Center for Architecture. Free.
Sunday 10/18
11:00 am: A bike tour of preservation wins. with the Young Friends of the Preservation Alliance, followed by a happy hour. $5.
Monday 10/19
5:30 pm: Learn the tricks of the neighborhood sustainability advocacy trade with Clean Water Action and PennFuture. $5, RSVP by 10/15.
