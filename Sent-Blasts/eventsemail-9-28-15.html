Each week the PlanPhilly staff curates our top picks of the week’s most informative, entertaining, and important events from our events page . Hosting an event? Submit it here and we’ll consider including it in our weekly email.
Monday 9/28
6:30 pm: Landscape urbanist Iñaki Echeverria delivers a lecture at PennDesign. Free.
Tuesday 9/29
7:30 am: Emily Bittenbender takes you through the hidden costs of constructing bidding at the second event in PIDC’s Construction Workshop series. Free.
9:00 am: Rob Viola, Vision Zero NYC’s senior project manager, is coming to present at DVRPC’s Regional Safety Task Force meeting. Free.
Wednesday 9/30
9:00 am: The proposal to add six more acres of land to the Wissahickon goes before Council’s Committee on Parks, Recreation, and Cultural Affairs. Free.
10:00 am: Camden food economy strategy and amendments to the Connections 2040 Long-range Plan are on tap at DVRPC’s board meeting. Free.
10:00 am: The Center City upzoning bill and several zoning remapping bills are up before City Council’s Rules Committee. Free.
12:00 pm: Head to Dilworth Park over your lunch break to see dance company PHILADANCO! and the Curtis Institute of Music’s Brass ensemble. Free.
12:30 pm: The BIA’s 14th annual housing conference, featuring a panel moderated by PlanPhilly Managing Editor Emeritus Matt Golas. $100 - $125.
5:30 pm: The Online Voter Registration Social at Emerald Street Urban Farm in Kensington celebrates the state’s new online registration tool. Free.
Thursday 10/1
8:00 am: Lizzie Woods from DRWC presents on the new Delaware River trail acquisition at Design Advocacy Group. Free.
10:00 am: Redevelopment Authority land transfers galore are up for votes in City Council. Free.
2:00 pm: The grand opening of the Pier 68 park on the Delaware in South Philly. Free.
Friday 10/2
6:00 pm: Mural Arts hosts the opening party for Open Source. $20.
Saturday 10/3
9:30 am: The Open Source North Trolley tour of recent Mural Arts projects. $35.
10:00 am: Paul Steinke hosts a Rail Park tour. $25.
10:00 am: The Revolutionary Germantown Festival features a Battle of Germantown reenactment, a beer garden, and family activites. Free.
11:00 am: A new Hidden City walking tour with GroJLart, “From Slaughterhouses to Skateparks: Exploring the Schuylkill Riverfront”. $8 - $12.
12:00 pm: North Philly Block Party with Open Source artists Keir Johnston and Ernel Martinez, with event partners AL DÍA News Media, WXPN, AfroTaino Productions, Raices Culturales Latinoamericanas. Free.
7:00 pm: The 19th annual Bowling Ball fundraiser benefitting the Community Design Collaborative. $65 +.
Sunday 10/4
2:00 pm: WHYY hosts a book release event for This I Believe: Philadelphia, featuring 30 essays from contributors to the original 1950s radio series, as well as 30 essays from WHYY’s more contemporary series. Free, but be sure to reserve your seat.
