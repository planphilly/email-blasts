Will you take 5 minutes to complete the PlanPhilly 2016 Reader Survey? It's been a while since we last surveyed our readers, and we know that since our move to WHYY, there are now a lot more people in our community. Help us better understand who you are, what you need from us, and what issues matter to you. Completed surveys will be entered for a chance to win a $50 Reading Terminal Market gift card.
Each week the PlanPhilly staff curates our top picks of the week’s most informative, entertaining, and important events from our events page . Hosting an event? Submit it here and we’ll consider including it in our weekly email.
Monday 1/25
6:30 pm: A 30-unit residence and a digital billboard/roof sign go before the Northern Liberties zoning committee. Free.
7:00 pm: The reuse plan for the PECO Power Plant goes before Fishtown zoners. Free.
Tuesday 1/26
7:00 pm: The Friends of Bainbridge Green in Queen Village are holding elections for 8 board seats. You can nominate candidates via email. Free.
Wednesday 1/27
8:00 am: The Green Stormwater Initiative quarterly meeting, and release of the Local Economic Impact Report for the Green City Clean Waters Initiative. Free for members, $25 for non-members.
6:00 pm: The Philly kick-off for Technical.ly and Comcast’s multi-city Tomorrow Tour, exploring how local tech networks can create stronger cities. Free.
7:00 pm: Get the latest news from South Street Headhouse District and the Italian Market BID committee at Bella Vista neighbors’ general meeting. Free.
Thursday 1/28
10:00 am: DVRPC’s board will vote on the final FY2017 Work Program and hear a presentation on Camden’s food economy. Free.
6:00 pm: ULI senior fellow Edwin McMahon delivers a lecture on “The Development Market and Green Infrastructure in US Cities and Suburbs" at Penn Design. Free.
Friday 1/29
8:30 am: A launch event and half-day forum for the latest edition of the Philadelphia Social Innovations Journal, hosted by Philadelphia Association of Community Development Corporations and Wells Fargo Regional Foundation. Free (SOLD OUT).
6:30 pm: An opening reception at Slought for "A Beautiful Ghetto”: Devin Allen’s “ visual story... about Freddie Gray's Baltimore and the rise of the New Activist.” Free.
7:00 pm: The Hive76 hackerspace hosts an Open House kick-off event for their new digs at Bok. Free.
Saturday 1/30
1:00 pm: Martin Bruckner discusses the rise of maps in 18th century American popular culture in a talk at Stenton $10-15.
2:00 pm: Winterfest at Starr Garden has lots of family-friendly entertainment. Free.
4:00 pm: Join Mural Arts, Al Aqsa, and ArtWell to help paint a new mural on the Al Aqsa Islamic Society building. Free.
