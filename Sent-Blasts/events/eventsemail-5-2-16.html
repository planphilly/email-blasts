Friday 5/6
This year would have been Jane Jacobs’s 100th birthday, and this Friday through Sunday, the international Jane’s Walk festival returns to Philadelphia with over a dozen free Jacobs-inspired walking conversations around the city. Friday’s Jane’s Walks include a Delaware River jaunt from 2nd and Tasker up to Pier 68, a tour of the City Hall area’s saddest public spaces, and a buffet of tactical urbanism projects in eastern University City. Free.
Saturday 5/7
Saturday’s Jane’s Walks include a tour of the smallest streets west of Washington Square, the urban and natural scenes of Mt. Airy, Germantown Avenue, old and new, landmarks from Philadelphia’s metropolitan ascent, the historic buildings of Sharswood, and the Schuylkill River Boardwalk and its environs. Swing by the Center / Architecture + Design anytime between 10 and 6 to refresh with some Jane Jacobs videos and meet other Jane’s Walk attendees. Free.
Sunday 5/8
Sunday’s Jane’s Walks includes tours of Franklin and Washington Squares by PlanPhilly editor Ashley Hahn, Rittenhouse and Logan Squares by Jim Saksa, a critical look at the Fairmount Avenue corridor, and a recap of recent Baltimore Avenue public space initiatives. Free.
