Each week the PlanPhilly staff curates our top picks of the week’s most informative, entertaining, and important events from our events page . Hosting an event? Submit it here and we’ll consider including it in our weekly email.
Tuesday 1/19
1:00 pm: The Planning Commission’s meeting will be a real doozer, featuring the 2012 Wharton development proposal, two 2035 District Plans, the 2015 Center City Parking Inventory, and the 1911 Walnut proposal for Rittenhouse Square. Free.
6:00 pm: Councilman Kenyatta Johnson’s office and PWD present plans for two rain gardens on vacant lots in Point Breeze. Free.
6:30 pm: A crash course on food and farming issues for city folks from PHS. Free, registration required.
Wednesday 1/20
3:45 pm: DVRPC hosts an afternoon forum on how technology and data are changing the game for community engagement in planning. Free.
6:30 pm: Snøhetta founding partner Craig Dykers speaks at PennDesign Free.
7:30 pm: Ask Councilman Mark Squilla some questions at Society Hill Civic Association’s monthly meeting. Free.
Thursday 1/21
8:00 am: Urban Land Institute hosts a breakfast forum at Villanova on the recent trends impacting office design. $45-60.
6:00 pm: The monthly Urban Geek Drinks happy hour at Frankford Hall. Free.
6:00 pm: The second public meeting for the Lower Southwest District Plan at Penrose School. Free.
7:30 pm: Queen Village Neighbors Association’s general meeting explores how to make the eastern side of South Street more of a place for neighbors. Free.
Friday 1/22
5:30 pm: Conrad Benner’s Streets Dept street art blog turns 5, and they’re hosting a party and gallery opening at Paradigm. Free.
Saturday 1/23
8:30 am: PHS hosts a basic training class for winter Tree Tenders. $25 fee.
