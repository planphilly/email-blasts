Each week the PlanPhilly staff curates our top picks of the week’s most informative, entertaining, and important events from our events page . Hosting an event? Submit it here and we’ll consider including it in our weekly email.
Monday 11/16
6:00 pm: Streetfilms, free beer, and Tactical Urbanism author Mike Lydon are at the first public event for Open Streets PHL. $5, registration required.
6:30 pm: East Kensington Neighbors Association is holding their board elections at Philadelphia Brewing Co. Nic Esposito of The Head & The Hand Press is running for President. Free.
Tuesday 11/17
5:00 pm: An exclusive tour of the new Cira Green elevated park with PennDesign Alumni Association. $10, registration required.
5:00 pm: Learn about the place-based apps Philadelphia city government uses to analyze and address problems like vacant buildings at Map, Measure, Manage. Free, reservation required.
6:30 pm: WHYY hosts a Speakeasy forum on police and community relations Free, registration required.
7:00 pm: Friends of the Bainbridge Green in Queen Village host a public comment party for Olin’s new new Bainbridge Green designs. Free.
Wednesday 11/18
9:00 am: The Delaware River Port Authority hosts their monthly board meeting. Free.
10:00 am: Penn GIS Day features presentations from Ira Goldstein, Sarah Cordivano, and friend of PlanPhilly Ken Steif. This year’s theme is Geography, Real Estate, and Civil Rights. Free, registration required.
4:30 pm: A reception for this year’s State of University City at World Cafe Live. Details.
7:00 pm: Mike Harris from South Street Headhouse District delivers some South Street updates at the Society Hill Civic Association general meeting. Free.
Thursday 11/19
6:00 pm: The monthly Urban Geek Drinks happy hour at Frankford Hall. Free.
6:00 pm: “From the Outside In: Suburban Elites, Third-Sector Organizations, and the Reshaping of Philadelphia.” Free.
6:30 pm: ‘Creepy Curiosities and Cocktails' with Young Friends of the Historical Society features a one night pop-up display featuring HSP’s most ghoulish items. $25-30..
7:00 pm: Queen Village Neighbors Association holds their annual board elections. Free.
Friday 11/20
7:30 am: This year’s ULI Real Estate Forecast features Dustin Downey, Director of Multifamily Development at Southern Land Co. Details.
8:30 am: Indy Hall’s Alex Hillman talks about work at this month’s Creative Mornings meet-up. Free.
Saturday 11/21
12:00 pm: The Water Department hosts a community paint day for the Heston Garden Mural. Free.
Sunday 11/22
4:00 pm: A mural re-dedication at Dirty Franks, celebrating the two recent dirty Franks that have been added: Pope Francis and Poet Laureate Frank Sherlock. Frank Sherlock will do a reading. Pope Francis will not. Free.
