Each week the PlanPhilly staff curates our top picks of the week’s most informative, entertaining, and important events from our events page . Hosting an event? Submit it here and we’ll consider including it in our weekly email.
Monday 4/4
11:00 am: City Councll’s parks committee considers an ordinance to acquire land for the Schuylkill River trail segment between South and Christian Streets. Free.
1:00 pm: City Council’s Licenses and Inspections committee considers an ordinance holding businesses accountable for nuisance activity on their premises. Free.
1:30 pm: DVRPC hosts a forum on ‘Parks that Reshape Cities.’ this is the event description. Free, registration required.
Tuesday 4/5
10:00 am: City Council hears testimony on the FY17 capital and operating budgets. Free.
1:00 pm: Bart Blatstein’s Broad and Washington tower goes back to Civic Design Review. Free.
5:30 pm: Sustainable Business Network presents ‘Steps Toward a No-Carbon House.’ St. Benjamin’s beer and snacks provided. Free, registration required.
6:00 pm: ‘Block by Block: Getting Things Done in Your Neighborhood’ hosted by Young Involved Philly. $10.
Wednesday 4/6
5:00 pm: Learn about Temple’s new library and related programs at “Building the 21st Century Library: Campus, City, Culture.’ Free.
6:30 pm: Phil Gilbert, GM of IBM Design, gives a talk at PennDesign. Free.
6:30 pm: Green Drinks Delaware Valley at Standard Tap. Free.
Thursday 4/7
8:00 am: Design Advocacy Group’s April meeting features an update on Germantown initiatives from Emaleigh Doley, Ken Weinstein, and Planning Commission staff. Free.
Friday 4/8
9:00 am: work&days is a one-day symposium at Penn on design careers in the 21st century. Free, registration required.
Saturday 4/9
10:00 am: South Kensington Community Partners holds a neighborhood clean-up, with barbecue afterward for volunteers. Free.
11:00 am: A PennDesign Alumni Association tour of Longwood Gardens $25, or $9 for Longwood Gardens members.
