Each week the PlanPhilly staff curates our top picks of the week’s most informative, entertaining, and important events from our events page . Hosting an event? Submit it here and we’ll consider including it in our weekly email.
Tuesday 9/6
1:00 pm: Civic Design Review Committee meeting. Public.
5:00 pm: Help clean up The Lakes at FDR Park. Public.
5:30 pm: "Public Art In Focus" Macro Photography Workshop With Stefan Suchanec. $5 for members and $15 for non-members.
7:30 pm: Bella Vista Neighbors Association zoning meeting. Cost.
Wednesday 9/7
9:30 am: Meeting of the Art and Architecture Committee of the Philadelphia Art Commission. Public.
6:30 pm: South of South Neighborhood Association zoning workgroup meeting. Public.
6:30 pm: The EwingCole Lecture: Wolf Prix at PennDesign. Public.
Thursday 9/8
8:00 am: Design Advocacy Group talks Circuit Trails with Sarah Clark Stuart (Bicycle Coalition) and Patrick Starr (Pennsylvania Environmental Council). Public.
12:30 pm: “Big Ecology in Small Places: Ecological Landscape Design for Residential Properties” at Stenton. Free, registration required.
3:30 pm: Philadelphia Land Bank Board Meeting. Public.
5:30 pm: DVRPC Public Participation Task Force meeting. Public.
5:30 pm: The Baltimore Avenue Dollar Stroll returns. $1 deals!
All Day: Greensgrow West’s grand opening at 5123 Baltimore Ave. Free.
Friday 9/9
9:00 am: Historical Commission meeting. Public.
6:00 pm: South Kensington Community Partners hosts “American Street Feast: A Celebration of our Neighborhood,” a night of conversation about the neighborhood (plus a potluck). Free, registration required.
Saturday 9/10
10:00 am: Wyck hosts a day of events as a part the Philadelphia Honey Festival. Free.
11:00 am: Enjoy a day on the lower Delaware and Schuylkill rivers as part of Pennsylvania Coast Day. Free.
12:00 pm: South Philly Sausage Fest, produced by the Newbold CDC. 100% of proceeds will fund the Newbold CDC’s Street Cleaning Program, which provides employment for Philadelphia residents with disabilities. $6 and up.
2:00 pm: A tour of the Tulpehocken Station Historic District, hosted by the Ebenezer Maxwell Mansion. Free.
Sunday 9/11
10:00 am: GreenFest Philly, an environmental festival, at Headhouse Square. Free.
