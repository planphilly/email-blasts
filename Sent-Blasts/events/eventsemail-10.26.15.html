Each week the PlanPhilly staff curates our top picks of the week’s most informative, entertaining, and important events from our events page . Hosting an event? Submit it here and we’ll consider including it in our weekly email.
Monday 10/26
10:00 am: Montgomery County hosts Scott Bricker and Chris Zimmerman of America Walks and Smart Growth America for a discussion of walkability and active transportation in MontCo. Free, registration required.
5:30 pm: The final public meeting on the Love Park redesign, hosted by PennPraxis. Free.
7:00 pm: Newbold Civic Association hosts a public meeting on the stalled plan for a Green City, Clean Waters parking lot makeover at Guerin Rec Center. Free.
Tuesday 10/27
4:00 pm: Urban Land Institute and the Knight Foundation present the findings of a national survey they conducted asking different generations what they want out of their neighborhoods. $25 members, $40 non-members.
Wednesday 10/28
All Day: The Design on the Delaware conference runs Wednesday through Friday, covering a panoply of sustainable energy and design initiatives from the city and non-profit sectors. Prices vary.
5:00 pm: ‘Tree Talk and Sappy Hour’ at Penn Treaty Park with the Forest Service, PHS, Parks & Rec, Fairmount Park Conservancy and Yards Brewing Co. Free.
6:00 pm: Drink some free beer and carve pumpkins in the sanctuary at Fleisher Art Memorial. Free.
No time specified: South Kensington Community Partners’ zoning committee reviews a number of significant development proposals. Free.
Friday 10/30
4:00 pm: Community Design Collaborative and DVAEYC host a day-long design charrette called ‘Child’s Play’ during Design on the Delaware. Cost.
7:00 pm: Learn about the Slave Dwelling Project from director Joe McGill, who’s been sleeping in extant slave dwellings for the past several years. $15 suggested donation.
Sunday 11/1
10:00 am: PennDesign hosts “Demystifying Design Professions: Perspectives on Diversity” Free, registration required.
