Each week the PlanPhilly staff curates our top picks of the week’s most informative, entertaining, and important events from our events page . Hosting an event? Submit it here and we’ll consider including it in our weekly email.
Monday 3/21
5:30 pm: Tyler Arts’s Crafts and Drafts monthly happy hour brings together Philadelphia working artists and creative professionals. Free.
6:00 pm: Kim Mathews and Signe Nielsen, Principals of Mathews Nielsen Landscape Architecture, whose work includes the East Market project, Hudson River Park, and Battery Park City, discuss their recent work at Penn Design. Free.
Tuesday 3/22
5:30 pm: DVRPC’s Public Participation Task Force discusses the future forces planners need to be thinking about. Free.
6:30 pm: Photographer and conceptual artist Hank WIllis Thomas gives a talk at PennDesign. Free.
Wednesday 3/23
8:00 am: Calling all architects: Join AIA PA for a trip to Harrisburg for Architects Action Day. Free.
5:30 pm: A book launch event at Fisher Fine Arts for “Shared Prosperity in America’s Communities” featuring Patrick Harker, President and CEO of the Philadelphia Fed, Eva Gladstein from the City of Philadelphia, Elizabeth Kneebone of Brookings’ Metropolitan Policy Program and more. Free.
6:00 pm: Women in Architecture: Heath and Wealth Workshop at WRT. $5-10.
7:00 pm: Center City Residents Associations hosts a zoning remapping meeting. Planning Commission members will be in attendance. Free.
Thursday 3/24
8:30 am: The Bicycle Coalition hosts a Vision Zero mini-forum at Jefferson. $10 for BCGP members, $45 for non-members.
10:00 am: The DVRPC Board makes recommendations for Transportation Alternatives Program project grants. Free.
6:00 pm: The monthly Urban Geek Drinks happy hour for civic do-gooders at Frankford Hall. Free.
6:30 pm: A premiere screening of 10 That Changed America at WHYY, and a panel discussion of William Penn's unlikely vision for Philadelphia and the fight for Fairmount Park. Free.
Friday 3/25
6:30 pm: Join WHYY’s Every ZIP Philadelphia project for an evening at Art Sanctuary, featuring live storytelling, music, and conversation. Free.
8:00 pm: The Secret Cinema and Philadelphia Parks and Rec present “Parks and Rec Rarities,” a screening of little-seen City-made films from the 1960s, '70s and '80s. $8.
Saturday 3/26
9:00 am: Help the WMAN Streetscapes Committee garden the Gateway at Lincoln Drive and Johnson St. in Mt. Airy. Free.
11:00 am: Join Philaphilia and Hidden City blogger GroJLart for a tour of Forgotten Chestnut Street. $12 Hidden City members, $18 non-members.
