Each week the PlanPhilly staff curates our top picks of the week’s most informative, entertaining, and important events from our events page . Hosting an event? Submit it here and we’ll consider including it in our weekly email.
Monday 3/7
1:00 pm: City Council’s Appropriations committee considers amendments to the 2016 capital program. Free.
6:30 pm: Philadelphia Water hosts two community meetings on Green City Clean Waters projects, one for Morris Park in West Philly, and another in Lawncrest at 7:15
7:00 pm: Alex Garvin, veteran of five NYC Mayoral administrations, gives a talk on “Sustainable Urban Planning in the Face of Shrinking Government Spending. Free.
Tuesday 3/8
10:00 am: The Vacant Property Review Committee, City Council’s check on Land Bank dispositions, holds their monthly meeting. Free.
Wednesday 3/9
9:30 am: The Art Commission considers the city’s new modern bus station designs, among several other projects. Free.
Thursday 3/10
9:30 am: DVRPC’s Healthy Communities Task Force hosts a discussion on planning for aging-friendly communities. Free.
10:00 am: City Council considers Council President Darrell Clarke’s resolution opposing parts of the Levy-Sweeney tax reform framework, now a bill in Harrisburg. Council’s Finance committee also considers amendments to the new green roof tax credit and an extension of the LOOP tax exemption. Free.
3:30 pm: The Land Bank board meeting agenda includes a “report on transfer of properties to Land Bank.” Free.
6:00 pm: Young Friends of the Preservation Alliance happy hour at Boot and Saddle. Free.
7:00 pm: Northern Liberties Neighbors Association hosts a public meeting on 5th Street traffic calming, with representatives from Councilman Darrell Clarke’s office. Free.
Friday 3/11
9:00 am: The Historical Commission reviews the playground rehab plans near the historic Bethel Burying Ground in Queen Village. Free.
7:00 pm: "The Food Axis: Cooking, Eating, and 1950s Domestic Architecture" at Cliveden. $15 suggested donation.
Saturday 3/12
12:00 pm: A tour of Penn’s Hill College House (34th/Walnut), designed by internationally renowned architect Eero Saarinen. Co-hosted by Young Friends of the Preservation Alliance and PennPreserve Alumni. Free.
1:00 pm: NKCDC hosts a lead information session about lead exposure in our area, and how to mitigate the health risks. Free.
