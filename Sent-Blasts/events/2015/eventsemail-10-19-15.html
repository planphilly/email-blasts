Each week the PlanPhilly staff curates our top picks of the week’s most informative, entertaining, and important events from our events page . Hosting an event? Submit it here and we’ll consider including it in our weekly email.
Before we get to the regular events schedule, here’s a quick reminder about our Neighborhood Civics 101 event this Wednesday, 10/21 for State of Young Philly. PlanPhilly reporter Jared Brey will moderate a conversation with members of some of Philly’s best-organized neighborhood civic groups about what civics do, what powers they have, and why Young Involved Philadelphians (and any of our readers!) should get involved with them. Tickets are going fast so grab one while they last.

12:30 pm: A walking tour of rarely-seen places in the Convention Center. $5.
5:30 pm: Learn the tricks of the neighborhood sustainability advocacy trade with Clean Water Action and PennFuture. $5.
Tuesday 10/20
10:00 am: Check out the city’s new Innovation Lab in the Municipal Services Building for State of Young Philly, and learn about how city government has become more innovative over the past few years. Free.

5:30 pm: A book talk from Peter Hendee Brown of the University of Minnesota on his recent book, How Real Estate Developers Think: Design, Profits, and Community, explaining the interests, motives, and actions of real estate developers. Free.
7:00 pm: PlanPhilly hosts Neighborhood Civics 101 at Ladder 15, with Matt Ruben, Penelope Giles, Emaleigh Doley, and Lee Tharps schooling the YIPsters on the neighborhood civic association landscape. $10.

10:00 am: Traffic forecasts and regional congestion management are a few of the topics on the agenda at DVRPC’s board meeting. Free.
12:30 pm: USA250 hosts a lunch meeting at City Hall with an update on their campaign to host the United States’ 250th birthday festivities in Philadelphia. Free.
4:00 pm: The Community Forum on Arts and Parks features Mayor Nutter, Kathryn Ott Lovell, Caroll Coletta, and others discussing Fairmount Park Conservancy’s participation in ArtPlace’s Community Development Investments program. Free.
5:30 pm: Debrief with the freshman committeepeople who won ward seats in 2014 at Ward Politics 2.0, moderated by our colleague Dave Davies. $10.
7:00 pm: The second public meeting for the North Delaware District Plan at Abraham Lincoln High School. Free.

Saturday 10/24

5:30 pm: Weigh in on the Love Park redesign at the final public meeting on the project. Free.
Tuesday 10/27
