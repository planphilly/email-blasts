Each week the PlanPhilly staff curates our top picks of the week’s most informative, entertaining, and important events from our events page . Hosting an event? Submit it here and we’ll consider including it in our weekly email.
Monday 3/28
1:00 pm: City Council holds a hearing evaluating the city’s strategy on improper mortgage foreclosures. Free.
6:30 pm: Potentially interesting zoning meetings in Northern Liberties and Lower Moyamensing. Free.
7:00 pm: Hannah Tran from the DNC is speaking at Newbold Civic Association’s monthly meeting on local opportunities with the convention. Free.
Tuesday 3/29
5:30 pm: PlanPhilly and Design Advocacy Group’s conversation with Anne Fadullon and Alan Greenberger is sold out, but if you want to take a chance at 6:30, we’ll let people in on a first-come-first-serve basis if there’s room. Free.
6:30 pm: Weigh in on street design proposals in the Tacony Connector Street study. Free.
Wednesday 3/30
5:30 pm: An opening party for the newly remodeled Center / Architecture + Design event space Free.
6:00 pm: Want to be a Philly 311 neighborhood liaison for your area? The city is hosting a training. Free, registration required.
6:00 pm: Peter Wallker, partner at PWP Landscape Architecture in Berkeley, delivers the annual Ian McHarg lecture at PennDesign. Free.
Thursday 3/31
10:00 am: City Council vote on Council President Darrell Clarke’s resolution opposing a major piece of the Levy-Sweeney tax reform bill. Free.
Friday 4/1
5:00 pm: The newly-formed Urban Consulate hosts their first City Lobby which is, “a pop-up parlor for urban exchange.” Free.
