Each week the PlanPhilly staff curates our top picks of the week’s most informative, entertaining, and important events from our events page . Hosting an event? Submit it here and we’ll consider including it in our weekly email.
Monday 2/22
1:00 pm: A hearing on public banking before City Council’s Commerce and Economic Development Committee. Free.
6:30 pm: A guest lecture from Florencia Pita, principal at FPmod, on #colorforming at PennDesign. Free.
7:00 pm: Get involved with Friends of Julian Abele Park in Graduate Hospital at their February meeting. Free.
Tuesday 2/23
1:00 - 7:00 pm: The NET/WORK 2016 job fair at The Fillmore. Free.
6:00 pm: A Tech in Motion tech networking mixer at U-Bahn. Free.
Wednesday 2/24
5:00 pm: Bloom Philly Festival: Sip and Skate with Center City District. Free.
6:00 pm: Become a Philly 311 Neighborhood Liaison. Free, registration required.
6:30 pm: Several big South Kensington projects up for a vote at the SKCP zoning meeting. Free.
6:30 pm: Creatures of the Night hike at Mount Moriah Cemetery. Free, email to register.
Thursday 2/25
10:00 am: DVRPC’s board meeting features a presentation of Brett Fusco’s 2045 Future Forces work, and an overview of economic development in Chester. Free.
5:00 pm: GoPhillyGo, Indego, and SPOKE Mag host a Multimodal Transportation Happy Hour at Fergie’s. Free.
5:15 pm: Famed public space thinker Jan Gehl is the keynote speaker at the 10th annual Edmund N. Bacon Awards. $22-150.
7:30 pm: Bart Blatstein presents his updated plans for Broad and Washington in Hawthorne. Free.
Saturday 2/27
7:00 pm: Casual Business is a rotating one-night-only exhibit hosted by a collective of up-and-coming Philly photographers. Free.
Sunday 2/28
10:00 am - 4:00 pm: The Cadence Community Bike Build at First Unitarian Church is a group effort to assemble over 100 donated bikes for local youth organizations $10 suggested donation.
