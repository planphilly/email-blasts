Each week the PlanPhilly staff curates our top picks of the week’s most informative, entertaining, and important events from our events page . Hosting an event? Submit it here and we’ll consider including it in our weekly email.
Mark your calendars! On August 10, PlanPhilly and Design Advocacy Group are hosting a special design-focused tour of the Navy Yard, guided by PIDC and Liberty Property Trust. Get your tickets today.
Monday 7/25
10:00 am: American Experiments, “an exhibit of ideas to improve democracy in the United States,” presented by the Committee of Seventy. Free, registration required.
Tuesday 7/26
8:30 am: “Opportunity, Poverty and National Policy: A Conversation at the 2016 Democratic National Convention” at the Temple Performing Arts Center. Free, registration required.
9:00 am: Architectural Committee Meeting. Public.
4:00 pm: (Another) open house regarding the recent funds allocated by the City to improve American Street, this time at the Crane Arts Building. Public.
6:00 pm: “The Economic Value of Green Infrastructure”, hosted by the DVRPC’s Public Participation Task Force. RSVP required by July 25th.
Wednesday 7/27
8:00 am: An AIA Philadelphia Housing Committee Meeting to discuss the future plans of the Land Bank in Philadelphia. Public.
9:00 am: The Penn Fels Policy Research Institute hosts a panel conversation about “Flint's water crisis and the role of academic researchers and media in preventing and learning from environmental disasters.” Free, registration required.
6:30 pm: Newbold Neighbors Association Meeting. Public.
Thursday 7/28
10:00 am: Delaware Valley Regional Planning Commission board meeting. Public.
7:00 pm: A screening of the documentary Kombit the Cooperative,” followed by a panel discussing environmental issues at the Awbury Arboretum. Free.
Saturday 7/30
10:00 am: Hidden City Philadelphia leads a “Forgotten South Broad Street” tour. $12-18.
