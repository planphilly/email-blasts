Each week the PlanPhilly staff curates our top picks of the week’s most informative, entertaining, and important events from our events page . Hosting an event? Submit it here and we’ll consider including it in our weekly email.
Tuesday 2/16
10:00 am: City Council’s Committee on Streets and Services considers regulations for “self-propelled mobility devices.” Free.
1:00 pm: The Planning Commission selects a new Chair and Vice-Chair, considers some large mixed-use projects and remapping plans. Free.
7:00 pm: Fishtown zoners consider a new brewery/restaurant with outdoor seating. Free.
Wednesday 2/17
6:00 pm: Shane Coen of Minneapolis firm Coen+Partners delivers a talk on “Contextual Minimalism.” Free.
6:30 pm: South Kensington zoning committee hears proposals for two multi-family buildings. Free.
Thursday 2/18
10:00 am: City Council considers resolutions transferring properties to the Redevelopment Authority and confirmations for City Solicitor, SEPTA board, and water rate-setting board. Free.
6:00 pm: Urban Geek Drinks, the monthly happy hour for civic do-gooders, is at Frankford Hall. Free.
6:00 pm: ‘Not Another Book Club’ discussion forum and happy hour, hosted by Philadelphia Emerging Architects, considers the best and worst of Nutter-era architecture. Free.
Friday 2/19
8:30 am: Natalie Nixon of Philadelphia University and INC.com gives a Creative Mornings talk on strategic and design thinking. Free.
2:30 pm: Take a tour of the FMC Tower construction site. Free, email to inquire.
4:00 pm: A policy forum on the relationship between transportation, energy consumption, and urban planning hosted by Penn IUR and the Kleinman Center for Energy Policy. Free.
Saturday 2/20
10:30 am: Learn about the urban ecology of three Delaware River piers on this walking tour. $10.
