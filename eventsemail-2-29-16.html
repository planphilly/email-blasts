Each week the PlanPhilly staff curates our top picks of the week’s most informative, entertaining, and important events from our events page . Hosting an event? Submit it here and we’ll consider including it in our weekly email.
Monday 2/29
5:30 pm: SOSNA hosts a Leap Day happy hour at Los Camaradas. Free.
6:30 pm: Liam Young of London-based urbanism and design think tank Tomorrows Thoughts Today gives a talk at PennDesign. Young was named one of 25 people who will change architecture and design by Blueprint magazine in 2010. Free.
7:00 pm: Newbold Civic Association’s monthly meeting covers their merger with Newbold CDC. They’re also requesting volunteers for zoning and other committees. Free.
Tuesday 3/1
7:30 am: “Contagious Urbanization: How Suburbs Are Embracing Mixed-Use and Density” hosted by ULI, moderated by Leigh Gallagher. Online registration closed, tickets available at the door.
10:00 am: Philadelphia Water is holding hearings this week on their proposed rate increase. Check out our events page for the other dates and locations. Free.
5:30 pm: Next City and Penn IUR host a discussion on health care, innovation, and the informal economy, followed by a reception Free.
6:00 pm: Tour the inside of the new Comcast Tower with Gensler and Philafad. $15.
Wednesday 3/2
10:00 am: City Council’s Public Health and Human Services committee holds hearings on smoking in multifamily buildings and Social Impact Bonds. Free.
1:00 pm: City Council’s Committee on the Environment holds a hearing on sustainable procurement practices. Free.
7:30 pm: Nate Hommel of Open Streets PHL presents at this month’s Nerd Nite. $5.
Thursday 3/3
8:00 am: Design Advocacy Group features Lindy Institute’s Harris Steinberg on Drexel’s work in University City. Free.
5:30 pm: “Philadelphia Television: Past, Present and Future” discussion at the Philadelphia History Museum. $10.
Friday 3/4
8:00 am: DVGBC’s 2016 Tri-State Sustainability Symposium is a one-day conference at Temple on best practices in the eco building industry. $10-60.
10:00 am: Ring in the spring planting season at Greensgrow Farms’s opening day kick-off. Free.
Sunday 3/6
2:00 pm: Hear the origin story of the Upper Roxborough Historic District at the Cedars. Free.
