Will you take 5 minutes to complete the PlanPhilly 2016 Reader Survey? It's been a while since we last surveyed our readers, and we know that since our move to WHYY, there are now a lot more people in our community. Help us better understand who you are, what you need from us, and what issues matter to you. Completed surveys will be entered for a chance to win a $50 Reading Terminal Market gift card.
Each week the PlanPhilly staff curates our top picks of the week’s most informative, entertaining, and important events from our events page . Hosting an event? Submit it here and we’ll consider including it in our weekly email.
Monday 2/1
7:00 pm: Fishtown neighbors talk property tax assistance and neighborhood safety initiatives at a joint meeting. Councilman Mark Squila will be in attendance. Free.
7:00 pm: Cobbs Creek Neighbors are hosting their inaugural meeting, focused on community planning. Free.
7:30 pm: Earn $100 helping Temple and SBNL with some photo mapping in the neighborhoods around Temple at this orientation meeting. Free.
Wednesday 2/3
6:30 pm: Green Drinks Delaware Valley at Standard Tap in Northern Liberties. Free.
7:00 pm: Graphic designer Shawn Hileman teaches a class on designing a successful infographic. $18 for Impart Creative members, $90 for non-members.
Thursday 2/4
8:00 am: Design Advocacy Group has Christine Knapp, the new director of the Mayor’s Office of Sustainability. Note the special off-site edition at Ballinger! Free.
SPONSORED - 5:30 pm: Learn about Temple University's Community and Regional Planning Graduate program, which explicitly focuses on sustainability, environmental planning, regional planning, and advanced computer applications. Free.
6:00 pm: PennDesign hosts a talk from 2015 ASLA Design Medal winner Thomas Balsley, who’s completed over 100 parks in New York City alone, as well as several famous parks nationwide. Free.
Friday 2/5
12:00 pm: A brown-bag lunch talk from Guy Thigpen, director of analytic services for the Philadelphia Land Bank. Free.
Saturday 2/6
10:00 am: Learn all about container gardening in small spaces at the Barnes Foundation. Pre-registration required, price unlisted.
11:00 am: A South Kensington meet-up hosted by Women’s Community Revitalization Project. Free.
11:30 am: The 2016 Distillery Run is a group run and whiskey tasting hosted by ODDyssey Half Marathon, with profits donated to Open Streets PHL. $25.
